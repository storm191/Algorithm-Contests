General Rules:

The contest is free and open to all, except final year BTech (Hons) and fourth year dual degree students of the Department of Computer Science and Engineering of IIT Kharagpur.

Register for the contest online.The registration opens more than a month before the contest and is open till a week before the contest. You require very simple contact details for registration.

A couple of days before the contest, we let you test our systems with toy problems. Participation in the Test-Run will ensure that you understand our systems better, and do not have to spend time on the day of the contest due to unexpected hitches.

On the day of the contest, we would make the problems public once the contest begins. We would also email you the problems in due course. Each problem is a programming assignment with a strong focus on algorithms and complexity.

For each problem, you have to work out a suitable algorithm and code it in C/C++ and submit it online. We provide sample inputs and outputs to make you better understand the problem statement.

A maximum of 10 submissions per problem is allowed.

You can post queries on our online forum and can talk to Admins also.

Winners will be contacted at the end of the contest by email to work out the logistics of the prizes.

All entries sent in will become the property of the CSE Department Society, IIT Kharagpur

The CSE Department Society, IIT Kharagpur reserves the right to modify the contest rules without any prior notice.

Evaluation Rules:

The submitted solutions will be tested on our servers with hundreds of test cases to exploit the boundary conditions in your code and check the robustness of your algorithm. Your solution will be evaluated on the basis of the number of test cases it successfully passes through.

Only the score of the last submission for each problem will contribute to a team's final ranking.

gcc/g++ compilers will be used to evaluate the solutions.

Only Math and STL libraries will be allowed. No other compile options (tags) will be used.

There will be a maximum execution time limit specific to each problem.

You can check the status of your solutions online and also find your team ranking to benchmark yourself with the rest.

Teams having identical source code or those submitting malicious code will be disqualified. Any attempt to hack, destroy or modify the servers by a team will entitle us to disqualify the team immediately from the contest.

In case of a tie in scores, the tie will be resolved using the total number of attempts of the tied teams

In case there is a tie in all respects, total prize money for the tied position will be equally shared by the tied teams.

Team composition:

Each team can comprise of a maximum of two members.

One person can be a member of at most one team. If we find the same person in more than one team, all the teams of which he/she is a member will be disqualified from the contest.

The team members need not necessarily be from the same college or company.

International Participants :

The T-shirts and the certificates to the winning participants will be mailed to them on their international address.
