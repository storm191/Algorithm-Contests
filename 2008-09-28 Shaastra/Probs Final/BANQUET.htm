Problems

1. Banquet

The king wants to hold his annual banquet, for which he wants to invite the rich and famous people in his country. Every potential guest has two integers associated, a money coefficient M and a fame coefficient F. A person will refuse to come if there is someone who is either richer or more famous than him. Formally a person A will accept an invitation to the party if and only if for all other invitees Bi, A is (strictly) richer than Bi or A is (strictly) more famous than Bi. Find the maximum number of guests who can be invited.

Input

The first line is T, the number of test cases. Then T cases follow. The first line of each case first contains N, the number of guests. The next line contains 2N integers, the ith pair is: Ri, Fi, viz. the money and fame coefficient of the guest i.

Output

A single line for each test case representing the maximum number of guests.

Constraints

1<=T<=20 1<=N<=20000 1<=Ri, Fi<=10000

Sample Input

4 3 20 30 40 50 30 40 4 20 30 10 10 30 20 40 50 3 10 30 20 20 30 10 4 10 10 20 30 40 50 39 51

Sample Output

1 2 3 2

Time limit: 1 second

Memory limit: 64 MB
