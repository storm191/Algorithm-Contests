Problems

5. Fight

You and your younger brother are fighting for the cookie box. Seeing this, your mother patches things up and imposes the following rule: Only one of you can remove one cookie at a time from either side of the box. The cookies in the box are of various weights (represented by integers). Both you and your brother want the maximum quantity (by total weight). You know that your brother will opt for a greedy strategy, i.e. he will take the biggest cookie from among the two sides. If both cookies are of equal weights, he will take one at the left end. Consumption is defined as the total weight of the cookies you have when the box is empty. Find the maximum difference in consumption of the cookies you can gain over your brother, given a cookie box configuration.
Assume that you make the first move.

Input

You are given many test cases. Each consists of a single line starting with N, the number of cookies. The subsequent N positive integers give the weights of the cookies in the cookie box. The last test case is followed by a single line containing a 0.

Output

For each test case output a single line containing the maximum difference in consumption of the cookies you can gain over your brother, for that cookie configuration.

Constraints

N does not exceed 1000. The sum of the numbers in the cookie box does not exceed 1000000.

Sample Input

4 3 2 10 4 8 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 8 2 2 1 5 3 8 7 3 0

Sample Output

7 4 5

Time limit: 1 second

Memory limit: 64 MB
