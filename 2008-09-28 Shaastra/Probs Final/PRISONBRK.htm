Problems

6. Prison Break

During a fierce battle, a number of prisoners of war have been held captive in an underground prison. Some of your comrades have also been imprisoned here. Your job is to plan a prison break. Fortunately, you have procured a secret document which is an X-ray aerial view of the prison. The image shows the prisoners as well as the boundaries of the walls of their cell. The image can identify your comrades. However it does not have depth perception, so you may not be able to identify which cell they are actually in. You are given that there is only one prisoner per cell. For example, in the image below...

Prisoner 1 may belong to cell 1, 2, or 3. However the correct assignment is Cell-1:4, Cell-2:1, Cell-3:2, Cell-4:3.

Input

The first line is T, the number of test cases. Then, T test cases follow.
A test case starts with a single line with the number N, the number of chambers.
Following these are N lines, Line i (1<=i<=N) contains 4 numbers X1, X2, Y1, Y2 where, X1<X2 and Y1<Y2. These denote the bounding coordinates of the rectangular cell "i". The following next N lines contain the coordinates of the prisoners. Line j (1<=j<=N) contains the X and Y coordinates, respectively of each prisoner "j".

Output

For each test case If there is ambiguity or no valid assignment, print 0 on a single line. Otherwise, print N lines each line containing integers A and B where A is the cell number and B is the prisoner number (1<=A, B<=N).

Constraints

You may assume no prisoner lies on the cell boundaries. Given N<=26. All coordinates fitin 32-bit signed integers.

Sample input

2 4 6 22 10 20 4 18 6 16 8 20 2 18 10 24 4 8 9 15 19 17 11 7 21 11 2 0 2 0 2 0 2 0 2 1 1 1 1

Sample output

1 4 2 1 3 2 4 3 0

Time limit: 1 second

Memory limit: 64 MB
